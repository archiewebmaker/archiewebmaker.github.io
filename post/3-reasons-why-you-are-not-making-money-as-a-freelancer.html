3 Reasons You're not Making Money as a Freelancer... and How to Turn it Around

  • Marc P Gangmei
  • August 14th, 2020
  • Make Money

When I started freelancing, I was so broke I struggled to buy an icecream for my kids. But now, I am less freelancing and am able to earn a full-time income from freelancing.

One of the biggest misconceptions about freelancing is that you sit at home and work comes to you. When the reality is you have to fight for it, and fight hard. I hate to break it to you, but working freelance means working. And I mean really working.

If you're not making money as a freelancer, it is your fault. The good news is that this is a fixable issue and if you decide to put in the work, you can change the situation around very quickly.

Here's why you're not making money as a freelancer and some quick and easy ways to fix it.

You are Searching for Clients in the Wrong Place

If you are finding that the clients you are attracting aren't the ones you were hoping to work withâ€¦ you might be looking for clients in the wrong place. Start by identifying who your ideal clients are, and the types of jobs do you most enjoy working on. Maybe you prefer a certain industry.

Don't assume you are not a robot, you can't work for a million people in a month - not even a hundred. Don't think of getting many clients. Instead, you may need to seriously pay attention to your price.

And taking on projects beyond your area of expertise is only going to cause you unnecessary and easily avoidable stress. I'm a experienced WordPress developer, but that doesn't mean I'll work on every single WordPress project that comes my way. I've curated a small list of tasks that are directly aligned with my specific skill set; those I know I can complete with extreme efficiency - custom design and user experience.

You're Relying on Websites such as Fiverr and Upwork

If you're relying on these sorts of freelancer websites to get work, let's be honest, you're missing out bigtime. You're hoping to put up a profile and get clients to find you and give you work. You apply for jobs, sure, but you're really not required to make much of an effort.

If you're serious about making an income, you need to dump the websites with the low-paying clientele and get serious about learning how to find and pitch clients directly. If you want to build a career that involves writing for national publications, doing content marketing for big brands, or making a good per hour rate, ditch the websites and start reaching out to editors and content managers directly.

Pitching is everything. How did I do it? You have to identify a client's need BEFORE you even reach out to them, offer them advice for free, and make a personal connection.

You're Not Marketing

If you're a new freelancer with little to no work, you simply have no excuse to not put every hour of freelancing time into marketing. That is all you should be doing all day, every day for as many hours as you can afford to.

If you want to make any kind of living with freelancing, you need to learn to get clients. And in order to get clients, you have to not only learn marketing, but learn how to market well, and learn how to market frequently.

My Golden Advice

I always told myself that if I figured out how to make it as a freelancer, I would help others do the same. An here it is - in this question: Why do you choose freelancing?

Honestly, I don't want to freelance all the time for the rest of my life. For me, it's simple - to live the dotcom lifestyle. Once you put your goal in place, things will become more clearer to you. Remember the old saying, "the rich don't work for money, they work for assets." Your assets should be building your own online audience, your community, your group - that's the secret! If you have 1 million active emails address, you will never need to work that much. Just think about what you can do with a 1 million social media subscribers. You can sell anything! Start investing in those areas alongside your freelancing!
