Pay Attention to Numbers: A Freelancer Story

Published on 1-08-20203 min readFreelancer

Hiring freelancers is becoming not only more acceptable, but more attractive for many businesses. This creates an incredible opportunity for people with useful skills to start a freelancing business on the side, and eventually grow that into a sustainable self-employed career. Are you a freelancer or thinking about getting into it? Here is a short story about a freelance blogger and freelance copywriter, enjoy and learn a few lessons!

Not so long ago, in a buzzing little town of Redactonburg, there lived two twins. They went to the same school and college.

One became a freelance blogger, the other a freelance copywriter. The blogger worked every day, but the copywriter worked only for seven days in a month. Surprisingly, the lazy copywriter earned much more than the blogger. "How can this be?" inquired Mr. Sherlock Holmes.

On investigation, it also found that the blogger received more clients than the copywriter in a month.

And in most of the cases, the copywriter got only one client in a month.

Finally, the final investigation report was on the desk.

The blogger charged $10 for 1000 words, whereas the copywriter charged $1000 for 1200 words. And the copywriter didn't work for amateurs and misers. He had the option to pick and choose only the juicy clients coming through his leaflets.

Here is the writing on the wall. Even if the blogger gets 30 clients in a month, it was impossible to earn half of what the copywriter earned in a single job.

P.S. This story didn't become the best selling story in town; to be honest, it was the blogger who wrote it.
